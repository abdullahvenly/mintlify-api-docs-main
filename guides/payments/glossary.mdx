## Platform Terms

### 2FA (Two-Factor Authentication)
Security feature requiring two forms of identification: password + time-based code from authenticator app.

### Account Verification
Process of confirming ownership of bank accounts or wallets before they can be used for transactions.

### APY (Annual Percentage Yield)
The rate of return earned on an investment over one year with compound interest.

### Business Approval
KYB verification process that confirms your business legitimacy before full platform access is granted.

### Dashboard
Main overview page showing key metrics, account status, and quick actions.

## Transaction Terms

### On-Ramp
Converting fiat currency (EUR, USD, etc.) into cryptocurrency. Also called "buying crypto" or "fiat-to-crypto."

**Example**: €5,000 EUR → 5,100 USDC

### Off-Ramp
Converting cryptocurrency into fiat currency. Also called "selling crypto" or "crypto-to-fiat."

**Example**: 12,800 USDC → €12,544 EUR

### Reference Code
Unique identifier for on-ramp transactions that must be included in bank transfers for payment matching.

**Format**: `ON-ABC12345`

### Transaction ID
Unique identifier for any transaction in the platform.

**Format**: `TXN-001`

### Processing Time
How long it takes for a transaction to complete:
- **On-Ramp**: 1-2 hours after bank transfer
- **Off-Ramp**: 1-2 business days

### Transaction Status
Current state of a transaction:
- **Transaction Created**: Just initiated
- **Waiting for Funds**: Awaiting bank transfer
- **Processing**: Being processed
- **Completed**: Successfully finished
- **Failed**: Error occurred

## Banking Terms

### IBAN (International Bank Account Number)
Standardized international bank account identifier.

**Format**: Country code (2 letters) + check digits + account number

**Example**: DE89370400440532013000

### BIC/SWIFT
Bank Identifier Code used for international transfers.

**Example**: INGBNL2A

### Bank Transfer
Moving money from one bank account to another. Also called "wire transfer" or "bank payment."

### Beneficiary
The person or business receiving a bank transfer. For on-ramps, this is typically PayRamp Solutions B.V.

### Verification Email
Email sent to confirm ownership of a bank account. Contains a link that must be clicked within 24 hours.

## Blockchain & Crypto Terms

### Blockchain
Distributed digital ledger that records all cryptocurrency transactions.

### Wallet
Digital account for storing, sending, and receiving cryptocurrency. Can be:
- **Managed**: Created and secured by Venly
- **External**: Your own wallet (MetaMask, Ledger, etc.)

### Wallet Address
Unique identifier for a crypto wallet, similar to a bank account number.

**Format**: 0x followed by 40 characters

**Example**: 0x742d35Cc6634C0532925a3b844Bc9e7595f8a3f

### Private Key
Secret code that proves wallet ownership. Never share this.

### Seed Phrase
12-24 words used to recover a wallet. Never share this.

### Gas Fees
Transaction fees paid to blockchain validators to process transactions. Varies by network:
- **Polygon**: $0.01 - $0.10
- **Ethereum**: $5 - $50
- **Avalanche**: $0.50 - $2

### Blockchain Network
The specific blockchain infrastructure. Supported networks:
- Polygon
- Ethereum
- Avalanche
- Binance Smart Chain (BSC)
- Arbitrum
- Optimism

### Confirmation
Validation of a transaction on the blockchain. More confirmations = more secure.

## Cryptocurrency Terms

### Stablecoin
Cryptocurrency pegged to a stable asset (usually USD) to minimize price volatility.

**Examples**:
- **USDC**: USD Coin
- **USDT**: Tether
- **DAI**: DAI Stablecoin
- **FRAX**: Frax

### USDC (USD Coin)
Most popular stablecoin, 1 USDC = $1 USD. Issued by Circle.

### USDT (Tether)
Largest stablecoin by market cap, 1 USDT = $1 USD. Issued by Tether.

### DAI
Decentralized stablecoin created by MakerDAO, 1 DAI = $1 USD.

### ETH (Ethereum)
Native cryptocurrency of the Ethereum blockchain. Not a stablecoin - price varies.

### MATIC (Polygon)
Native cryptocurrency of the Polygon network. Not a stablecoin - price varies.

### Cryptocurrency
Digital or virtual currency secured by cryptography and recorded on a blockchain.

## DeFi Terms

### DeFi (Decentralized Finance)
Financial services built on blockchain technology without traditional intermediaries.

### Yield
Returns or interest earned on crypto holdings. Expressed as APY.

### Vault
Smart contract-based investment strategy that generates yield on deposited assets.

### TVL (Total Value Locked)
Total amount of assets deposited in a DeFi protocol or vault.

### Protocol
Set of rules and smart contracts that power a DeFi application.

**Examples**: Aave, Compound, Curve, Yearn, Convex

### Liquidity Pool
Collection of funds locked in a smart contract used for trading, lending, or other purposes.

### Lending Protocol
DeFi platform where users lend assets to earn interest (Aave, Compound).

### Yield Farming
Strategy to maximize returns by moving assets between different DeFi protocols.

### Smart Contract
Self-executing code on a blockchain that runs when conditions are met.

### Audit
Security review of smart contract code by independent experts.

## Compliance Terms

### KYB (Know Your Business)
Verification process to confirm business identity and legitimacy.

### KYC (Know Your Customer)
Identity verification process for individuals.

### AML (Anti-Money Laundering)
Regulations and procedures to prevent money laundering.

### Compliance Monitoring
Automated checks on all transactions to ensure regulatory compliance.

### Verification
Process of confirming identity, ownership, or authenticity.

## Fee Terms

### Processing Fee
Platform fee for handling transactions:
- **On-Ramp**: 0.5%
- **Off-Ramp**: 1.0%

### Currency Tier Fee
Fee based on currency liquidity and market:
- **Tier 1**: 0.30% - 0.45%
- **Tier 2**: 0.45% - 0.55%
- **Tier 3**: 0.60% - 0.70%

### All-In Pricing
Total cost shown upfront, including all fees.

### Volume Discount
Reduced fees for businesses with higher monthly transaction volumes.

### Performance Fee
Fee on earned interest from yield vaults (10-15% of profits only).

## Exchange Terms

### Exchange Rate
Ratio at which one currency converts to another.

**Example**: 1 EUR = 1.02 USDC

### Rate Lock
Exchange rate is fixed when transaction is confirmed, protecting from market fluctuations.

### Spread
Difference between buy and sell prices. We use market rates with no markup.

### Slippage
Price difference between expected and actual execution. Minimized with rate lock.

## Risk Terms

### Risk Level
Classification of investment risk:
- **Low**: Safest, lower returns
- **Medium**: Moderate risk and returns
- **High**: Higher risk, higher potential returns

### Impermanent Loss
Potential loss from providing liquidity in DeFi, due to price changes.

### Smart Contract Risk
Possibility of bugs or vulnerabilities in code, despite audits.

### Market Risk
Risk from market volatility affecting asset prices or yields.

### Liquidity Risk
Risk that assets cannot be quickly converted without affecting price.

## Status Terms

### Verified
Account has passed verification and is ready to use.

### Active
Wallet or account is operational and ready for transactions.

### Pending
Awaiting review, confirmation, or processing.

### Failed
Transaction or verification encountered an error.

### In Progress
Business verification or process is currently being reviewed.

### Approved
Business has passed KYB verification and has full platform access.

## Technical Terms

### API (Application Programming Interface)
Interface allowing software systems to communicate. Available for enterprise customers.

### Webhook
Automated message sent when specific events occur.

### Session
Period of active use after logging in. Expires after inactivity.

### Cache
Temporary storage of data for faster loading. Clear cache to resolve some issues.

### Browser Cookie
Small data file stored by websites to remember information.

### Incognito/Private Mode
Browser mode that doesn't save history or cookies. Useful for troubleshooting.

## Support Terms

### Ticket
Support request submitted to customer service.

### Response Time
How quickly support responds:
- **Standard**: Within 24 hours
- **Security**: Within 2 hours
- **Enterprise**: Priority support

### Escalation
Moving a support issue to higher-level team for resolution.
